1)Remove the cap from a clean plastic bottle. Use scissors to carefully poke some small drainage holes in the base of the bottle.

2)Use your scissors to make two level slits on opposite sides of the bottle,
near to the base.

3) Push a stick through the holes. There should be around 5 centimetres of stick left outside the bottle on each side

4) Slightly above each perch, use the scissors to
cut a feeding hole in the shape of an arch.

5) Create a second set of perches and feeding holes: repeat steps 2 to 4.

6) Use the scissors to make two holes in the neck of the bottle, on opposite sides and level with each other. Widen these with scissors

7) Decorate your bird feeder with the sticky back plastic provided.

8) Thread the string through the holes, then fill the bottle with bird food and replace the bottle cap.

9)Find a sheltered location outside to hang your feeder and wait for your birds to arrive! It might take a few days for this to happen.
